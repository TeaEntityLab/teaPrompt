## Required Wording Changes

These are corrections to the survey framing, upstream claims, or any future clean-
room experiment. None was applied to a governed TeaPrompt operating surface or to
the external documents.

1. **Snapshot scope:** call each hash a point-in-time content pin, not an immutable
   revision or provenance guarantee.
2. **Traceability:** say “link each derived requirement to its source and current
   lifecycle state,” not “raw wording is sacred.” NASA-style traceability is a
   structural association; it does not require verbatim text to override current
   validated requirements.
3. **Requirement changes:** append-only history needs stable requirement IDs and
   explicit `active` / `superseded by REQ-NNN` state. “Follow the final version”
   is not a deterministic state model.
4. **Privacy and authority:** source references must be scoped, classified,
   redactable, correctable, and deletable where policy requires. User/source text
   remains data and scoped intent, not higher-order instruction authority.
5. **Long-document claims:** use “coverage-oriented distillation” rather than
   “without deletion” or “all points intact.” Character ratio and semantic
   preservation are orthogonal; model self-report and point counts prove neither.
6. **Coverage proof:** a meaningful coverage check needs a pre-distillation
   manifest, source-point-to-output mapping, explicit missing/contradictory status,
   and human fidelity review. The source defines none of these.
7. **Private notebooks:** agent-authored experience must remain non-authoritative
   evidence until provenance, authority class, review, expiry, correction,
   conflict, rollback, and human-approval gates pass.
8. **Board semantics:** call the shared Markdown file a human-paced teaching
   convention, not a queue. It has no append lock, message IDs/cursor, typed state,
   acknowledgement, idempotency, cancellation, failure detection, or freshness
   bound.
9. **Archive behavior:** resolve the contradiction between “append only; never
   edit/delete” and cutting completed entries into an archive. A crash between
   removal and archive persistence creates a direct data-loss path.
10. **Role updates:** editing a role guide and announcing the change are two
    independent writes, not an atomic action. Active workers need versioning,
    delivery, acknowledgement, rollback, and reconciliation.
11. **Restart claims:** local files preserve prose, not necessarily effective
    state. Correct resumption also needs current-version selection, checkpoints,
    seen-message state, partial-write recovery, and workspace reconciliation.
12. **Routing:** replace the under-30-minute bypass with task-shape signals:
    independence, state, risk, coordination cost, failure cost, privacy, and
    available host enforcement.
13. **Packaging:** call the compact page source material for manual adaptation,
    not a released skill package. It has no named `<skill-name>/SKILL.md`
    distribution, document-level license, version, changelog, or tested discovery
    behavior.
14. **Novice claim:** disclose the actual operator burden: multiple terminal
    sessions, polling, health diagnosis, ownership enforcement, partial-state
    reconciliation, and failure recovery.

## Shared Findings

### What the Concept Gets Right

1. **Telephone-game risk is real in mechanism.** Every handoff or compression step
   can detach a derived requirement from its source. A durable source reference
   makes disagreement inspectable.
2. **Completion should close against intent, not activity.** Checking must-do items,
   red lines, and unresolved questions is stronger than accepting worker reports
   at face value.
3. **Stable contract and accumulated experience are different artifacts.** Role
   identity should not be rewritten whenever one new lesson appears.
4. **Explicit unknowns matter.** Recording pending interpretation before work is
   safer than silently filling a gap.
5. **Human-readable local artifacts aid inspection.** Markdown can be a useful
   brief, ledger, handoff, or audit record when it is not misrepresented as a
   transactional runtime.
6. **File/slice ownership is a useful precondition.** Distinct ownership reduces
   conflict, even though prose cannot enforce it.
7. **The source labels its end-to-end board example as invented.** That disclosure
   is better provenance than presenting a synthetic run as observed evidence.

### Load-Bearing Gaps

1. **No outcome evidence.** The “real team” anecdotes provide no inputs, outputs,
   dates, models, logs, comparison, error rates, or selection criteria. The only
   complete board example is explicitly invented.
2. **Mutable and undated sources.** The hashes pin the reviewed text but cannot
   reveal prior edits or future drift.
3. **No document-level license.** Platform-level license choices do not establish
   a chosen license for these pages.
4. **Structural traceability is conflated with raw-text authority.** Source wording
   can justify a derived requirement without overriding system/project policy,
   coordinator scope, later corrections, or privacy obligations.
5. **Effective requirement state is missing.** Retaining old and new wording does
   not tell a worker which requirement is active or what superseded it.
6. **The board is not a queue.** Concurrent whole-file read/modify/write appends
   can overwrite one another; no runtime primitive prevents loss or duplication.
7. **Append-only and archive-cut rules conflict.** The advertised invariant is
   broken by the manager’s own completion procedure.
8. **Delivery is polling by interpretation.** There are no message IDs, addressees,
   typed entries, cursors, freshness bounds, or acknowledgements.
9. **Restart recovers identity prose, not current state.** Progress, partial writes,
   current requirement version, seen messages, and workspace reconciliation are
   unspecified.
10. **“Atomic” updates are two writes.** Role-guide modification and board notice
    can be split by failure or observed in different orders.
11. **The manager is a bottleneck and single point of failure.** Centralized
    judgement can be useful; routing every question, update, and completion
    through one session is not resilient coordination.
12. **Role creation bypasses governance.** First-line identity and self-maintained
    guides have no registry, collision guard, provenance class, approval, test,
    version, propagation, rollback, demotion, or retirement rule.
13. **Private notebooks are self-modifying prompt surfaces.** Append-only
    experience can preserve hallucinated or poisoned lessons and later be read as
    authority.
14. **“No-loss” distillation is undefined.** The compact skill is 21.1% of the
    detailed article by character count; that ratio neither proves nor disproves
    semantic preservation. No canonical “point” or coverage map exists.
15. **Contradiction and missing-information handling are absent.** A distillate can
    retain a source faithfully while retaining its contradictions or hiding its
    omissions.
16. **Topology is fixed where it should be selected.** One manager plus three
    workers plus two support roles can overstaff small work and undersupply broad
    work.
17. **Elapsed time is a weak route.** A 30-minute estimate does not measure
    independence, coordination, risk, failure cost, privacy, or sequentiality.
18. **Novice framing under-discloses operational skill.** Diagnosing stalled
    sessions, races, stale state, partial writes, and recovery is not beginner
    work.
19. **The artifact contract carries most value.** A single agent using a brief,
    state ledger, sufficiency gate, and review-gated handoff captures the useful
    methodology without six-session runtime ceremony.

## Disagreements / Residual Risks

- **Adopt DJ2 now versus defer:** the requirements lens argued that a missing source
  field is a verified structural gap and that the recurrence gate does not veto
  in-place repairs. The panel retained that rule but rejected the conclusion:
  exemption from a promotion gate permits a justified repair; it does not prove
  that every absent field is a defect. Current contracts do not claim verbatim
  lineage, and no observable local failure establishes the expansion’s value.
  DJ2 therefore remains deferred with an exact first-incident trigger.
- **Requirement lifecycle status:** the same lens found no `active` / `superseded`
  field locally. The absence is real, but no local requirement-change failure was
  found. It remains concept/reference under DJ1 rather than a silent scope
  expansion.
- **DJ6 deferred versus no-change:** the memory lens recommended rejecting a
  “no-loss verifier” outright because semantic completeness is not mechanically
  decidable as framed. Synthesis rejects the no-loss claim now but keeps a narrow
  deferred route for a pre-enumerated coverage manifest plus human fidelity
  review after a verified local omission.
- **Canonical no-change wording:** the role-governance lens proposed adding
  DilinAI-specific prohibitions to `artifact-promotion.md` and
  `runtime-trust-boundary.md`. Synthesis keeps those surfaces general: they
  already require authority/provenance/approval/retirement and already prevent
  source text from overriding project rules. This record is the durable
  DilinAI-specific no-change decision.
- **Reproduction wording:** the pages are detailed enough to inspire an independent
  teaching setup, but their outcomes are not reproducible evidence. “Reproduce =
  no” refers to effectiveness/reliability, not the physical ability to create
  several terminals and a Markdown file.
- **Skill packaging wording:** the page is not distributed as a named skill
  directory/release and discovery was not tested. Frontmatter absence is a
  portability weakness, but this survey does not claim every host necessarily
  rejects a manually adapted file.
- **Simple-board steelman:** with one present human serializing writes, independent
  low-consequence slices, short duration, and tolerance for rework, the board can
  be a legible teaching aid. Those assumptions negate the stronger “safely let
  go” and dependable-recovery claims.
- **Provider fallback:** two panel backends failed before producing evidence. The
  successful reviewer batch and follow-ups supplied all seven full lens
  contracts; failed outputs were excluded rather than counted as dissent.

## Falsifiability

This no-change decision is wrong if any of the following occurs and the Candidate
Adoption Ledger is not re-opened:

1. A TeaPrompt plan, retrospective, incident, or panel record shows that a derived
   requirement or acceptance criterion lost its source linkage, diverged from the
   user’s actual intent, and caused rework or a wrong deliverable. This promotes
   DJ2 to an in-place repair.
2. A local role/scaffold or handoff failure survives the existing brief, state-
   ledger, provenance, promotion, review, and retirement controls, and a dedicated
   memory-owner artifact measurably prevents recurrence. This re-opens DJ3–DJ5
   without bypassing frozen-core or human-approval gates.
3. A local long-document task loses a load-bearing item that current source,
   contradiction, missing-information, State Ledger, and Sufficiency Gate checks
   do not catch, while a pre-enumerated coverage manifest would catch it. This
   re-opens the narrow manifest form of DJ6; it does not prove lossless semantics.
4. A deterministic coverage unit and mechanical point-loss check are defined,
   with human fidelity review and adversarial tests, making the DJ6 contract
   falsifiable rather than model-judged.
5. Project direction explicitly retires the multi-agent-runtime standing non-goal
   and a host implementation proves locked writes, typed task/message state,
   delivery/acknowledgement, idempotency, cancellation, permissions, checkpoint
   recovery, current-version selection, and adversarial concurrency/restart
   behavior. This re-opens DJ7.
6. The external author publishes a copy-permitting document-level license,
   packaged pinned release with version/changelog, security model, and preserved
   real-task trials. This moves DJ8 from blocked to eligible for a clean-room
   evaluation; it does not by itself justify TeaPrompt adoption.
7. A controlled trial on a host without native team primitives shows the proposed
   board pattern has no lost/duplicate entries under unsynchronized appends,
   correct archive/restart recovery, and lower total cost or higher task success
